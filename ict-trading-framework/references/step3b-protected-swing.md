# Step 3B: Protected Swing

## Definition

Protected swing = most recent swing high/low that HAS NOT been violated after entry

## Key Rules

- Stop loss placed beyond protected swing (structure-based)
- Bullish: protected swing = most recent higher low (HL)
- Bearish: protected swing = most recent lower high (LH)
- Once new structure forms → trail stop to new protected swing
- Protected swing MUST hold for trade validity
- Break of protected swing = potential reversal

## Protected Swing Quality

- Swing must be clear and visible (not micro/noise)
- Swing must align with structure (HL in bullish, LH in bearish)
- Avoid internal minor swings in choppy market
- Prefer swing formed after displacement (stronger validity)

## Stop Placement Logic

| Approach | Placement | R:R |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| Conservative | Beyond swing low/high of pullback | Safer |
| Aggressive | Beyond recent wick/small structure | Higher |

- Never place stop inside FVG
- Stop must be beyond level that confirms trade wrong

## Decision Logic

1. Set initial stop beyond protected swing
2. As price moves favorably → new swings form
3. Trail stop to each new protected swing
4. Stop moves closer to BE then profit
5. Exit when protected swing broken OR target hit

## Continuation Validation

- Protected swing holds = continuation valid
- New protected swing forms = trail opportunity
- Price respects swing as S/R = strong continuation
- No new protected swing = potential stagnation

## Entry Readiness

- Protected swing clearly identifiable
- Protected swing passes quality filter
- Stop placement fits risk parameters
- Path to target clear
- If too close → low R:R → skip

## Connection to Previous Steps

- Step 1: Target (liquidity)
- Step 2: Entry trigger (CISD/MSS)
- Step 3: Entry zone (FVG)
- Step 3B: Stop logic + continuation tracker

## Works When

- Clear swing structure
- Swing formed after displacement
- Protected swing holds as S/R
- Price progressing toward target
- New protected swings forming

## Fails When

- Protected swing broken
- No clear swing structure
- Micro/noise swing used
- Protected swing too close to entry
- Multiple swings broken quickly

## No Trade Conditions

- Protected swing unclear
- Protected swing fails quality filter
- Protected swing too close to entry
- Break before entry
- Protected swing conflicts with FVG invalidation

## Note

- Protected swing = structural stop
- Focus on CLOSE beyond swing, not wick
- Protected swing defines "trade is wrong"
- Trail stop = lock profit, not predict reversal
